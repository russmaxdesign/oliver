Oliver Gregg: Prop maker

About Oliver

I'm Oliver Gregg, a prop maker and special effects artist studying at the National Institute of Dramatic Arts in Sydney. This website allows me to share my creations and the skills and techniques I've learned while making my props.

Recent projects

Semi-acoustic guitar build

For my industrial technology major work in high school I created a semi-acoustic electric guitar out of native Australian timbers. Throughout the creation of this project I used several woodworking techniques such as steam bending, kerfing and hollowing of timber.

Zombie Door

An early project was to replace the existing walls and door of a cupboard with zombie apocalypse themed panels and latches. The walls were created with a mixture of plaster, shellac, paint and sand.

Aluminium hunting knife

This hunting knife prop was created out of 6mm aluminium, scrap wood and rope. The aluminium blade was polished and blunted to avoid trouble.

Aboriginal spear and woomera (spear thrower)

For this project I aimed to create a aboriginal spear and woomera using only traditional methods, this involved me finding the materials myself, shaping and carving them by hand and using vine and bark mixed with spinifex resin to attach elements.

Aztec inspired fantasy weapons

I created an incredibly oversized macuahuitl out of foam, with black acrylic spikes with an accompanying acrylic sickle.

Self portrait

A large scale self portrait of myself in the artstyle of Deb Weir, created with simple acrylic paints and artline markers

Yara-ma-yha-who sculpture

A sculpture I created of the Aboriginal Australian creature recounted from the stories of David Unaipon, created from a 3d printed 3d model I sculpted which was then coated with several layers of silicone paint to replicate skin texture.

Club toilet stall

A grotty and grimy toilet cubicle created entirely (excluding the real toilet) from mdf, brown paint and posca pens.
